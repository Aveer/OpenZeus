## Temperament

Boston Terriers are:
- **Friendly** — Love meeting new people and other animals
- **Intelligent** — Quick learners, eager to please
- **Playful** — Natural comedians, love to entertain
- **Affectionate** — Bond strongly with family
- **Sensitive** — Don't respond well to harsh training

### Personality Traits

- Gentle and well-mannered indoors
- Good with children when properly socialized
- Can be stubborn at times — patience is key
- Prone to separation anxiety — don't leave alone long
- Alert and makes a decent watchdog (but not aggressive)

## History

- **Origin**: Boston, Massachusetts, USA (late 1800s)
- **Ancestry**: English Bulldog + White English Terrier
- **Original purpose**: Originally bred for pit fighting, evolved into companion dog
- **AKC recognized**: 1893 — first US breed recognized by AKC

## Care Guide

### Exercise
- Moderate exercise needs: ~1 hour per day
- Enjoy short walks, fetch, and playtime
- **Avoid**: Overexertion in heat (brachycephalic = flat-faced)
- Best exercised in cool parts of the day

### Grooming
- Short coat sheds minimally
- Weekly brushing to remove loose hair
- Regular nail trimming
- Clean facial folds to prevent skin issues
- Occasional baths only

### Diet
- Prone to obesity — monitor food intake
- High-quality dog food, appropriate portions
- Avoid over-treating during training

### Training
- Eager to please — responds well to positive reinforcement
- Sensitive nature — use gentle, motivational methods
- Early socialization recommended
- Can get bored with repetitive training — keep it fun!

---

## Health Concerns

### Common Issues (Brachycephalic Breeds)
- **Brachycephalic Syndrome** — breathing difficulties due to short snout
- **Heat sensitivity** — cannot regulate temperature well
- **Eye problems** — prominent eyes prone to injuries/irritations
- **Patellar luxation** — trick knee (common small dog issue)
- **Skin allergies** — can suffer from environmental/food allergies

### Tips
- Regular vet check-ups
- Keep in air-conditioned environment in hot weather
- Avoid strenuous exercise in humidity
- Monitor weight closely
